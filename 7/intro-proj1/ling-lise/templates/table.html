H.S. Policy Debate Statistics in the United States

Policy Debate is a form of debate that involves four people debating in pairs. It requires much research and evidence 'cutting' , a term commonly used to denote the searching of pieces of evidence for a particular argument. Each pair is either affirmative -> affirming a certain plan pertaining to one year's topic or negative -> negating the aff's plan arguing why the affirmative's plan sucks. Each year, a new topic is chosen (for example space and transportation infrastructure); and the aff's plan must pertain to that topic.


You can find more information on policy debate in the wikipedia's site on policy debating OR visit the Stuy Speech and Debate Team for more information.
